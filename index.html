This research includes the evaluation of openings in different rating ranges and openings from chess.com. the research's aim is to get the hang of tension by the end of the opening fazes in chess. I gave 1 or 2 tension points to each player based on the criteria. Then I evaluated the percentages of each opening and rating range. I hope you enjoy this informative page because this took me a lot of time :) . The criteria used throughout this research is provided below:

  • For each one of 4 center squares controlled: +2
  • For each piece developed from its starting position: +1
  • For castling: +2
  • For each possible (no further than 1 move after) or present attack: +1
  • For each pin (pin would count only if there is a possibility of opponent moving away that piece which would result in material loss, unless that piece already interacts with the attacking piece): +2

Findings:

100-800 elo:

general facts:

  • Games where all of the center squares where controlled: ~49%
  • Games where only one side castled: ~32%
  • Both sides castled in ~7% of total games. (Therefore neither side castled in ~61% of games included in the study)
  • At least 1 pin was present in ~27% of games.
  • Games where 6 or more pieces were developed was ~29%
  • At least 2 attacks present: 16%
  • Average combined tension points for each game: 13.79
